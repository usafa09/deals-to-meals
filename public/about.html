About Dishcount

We believe eating well shouldn't mean spending more.

Our Mission
To flip meal planning on its head. Instead of picking recipes and hoping the ingredients fit your budget, we start with what's on sale at your local stores and build meals around the savings. Every family deserves to eat well without overpaying.

The Problem We Solve

Studies show the average American family spends roughly $270 per week on groceries (USDA, 2025) — and throws away nearly 30% of what they buy (USDA Economic Research Service). Meanwhile, every grocery store runs weekly sales that most shoppers never fully take advantage of.

Dishcount bridges that gap. We pull this week's deals from 80+ grocery chains, show you what's actually on sale at YOUR local stores, and build personalized meals around those savings — so your meal plan and your budget work together, not against each other.

How It Works

1
Enter your zip code and we'll find every grocery store near you — along with their current weekly sales.
2
Browse this week's deals across all your favorite stores. Tap items you want to cook with, and mark any you'd rather skip.
3
Dishcount builds your weekly meal plan — 5 personalized recipes built around this week's savings tailored to the items on sale, your dietary preferences, and your chosen cooking style — so you eat great and save money.

Our Commitment

Dishcount is free to use. We don't sell your data, we don't take a cut of your grocery bill, and we never will. Our goal is simple: help families eat better for less.

What Makes Us Different

Most meal planning apps start with recipes and send you to the store hoping you can afford it all. Most deal apps show you sales but leave you to figure out what to cook.

Dishcount is the only tool that connects both — real weekly sale prices from your local stores, combined with AI that knows your family's size, tastes, dietary needs, and schedule. The result: personalized meals that save you real money every week, with the math to prove it.

Built in Dayton, Ohio

I built Dishcount because I was tired of the same cycle — plan meals, go to the store, wince at the total. The fix seemed obvious: start with what's on sale, then figure out what to cook. What began as a side project for my own family is now a free tool for yours.

— Bill McCormick, Founder
