Meals, Deals & Lessons from the Kitchen Table

Honest, practical writing about meal planning and grocery savings — no fluff.

Welcome to the Dishcount blog.

I'm Bill McCormick, a dad in Dayton, Ohio, and I built Dishcount because I got tired of paying full price for groceries I could've bought on sale. This is where I write about the stuff I've learned along the way — how to read a weekly ad, what's worth buying at ALDI versus Kroger, cheap dinners my family actually eats, and the occasional story about what it's been like building a product from my kitchen table.

I'm not a chef. I'm not a finance guru. I'm just someone who figured out that the way most of us shop for groceries is backwards, and that flipping it saves real money. Everything here is written from that angle — honest, practical, no fluff.

New posts go up every week or so (depending on kids' sports schedules). If you've got something you want me to write about, or you disagree with something I've said, the contact page goes straight to my inbox. I'd rather hear from you than not. I want to make this site as useful to as many people as possible!

— Bill McCormick
